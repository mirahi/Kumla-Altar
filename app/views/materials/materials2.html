FTIR

According to archival material, Kumla altar was previously conserved in the late 1950-s. Wax or most probably a wax-resin mixture was used to consolidate the loose ground, gilding and paint layers.

The waxy mixture was removed as much as possible from the surface during this project and it was analyzed, using FTIR (Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy) analysis.

The absorption spectrum obtained, relates very closely to candelilla wax, a vegetable wax with a melting point around 68.5–72.5 °C, soluble in many organic solvents.
