Gilded tin flower

One of the interesting elements found as decoration on the Kumla altar-piece is the gilded tin flower.

It was originally used as a row of flowers, each in the middle of roundels in the dividing border between the upper and lower painted scenes of the Passion of Christ.

The borders are painted with red and black and the areas between the roundels with tin flowers are decorated with a leaf motive in silver with a copper green glaze.

There are not a single complete flower left on the altar but there are all in all 3 incomplete flowers still left at the very edges of the dividing border, two of them on the border between the two painted scenes of Passion - The Flagellation and the Deposition from the Cross.
