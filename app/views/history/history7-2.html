Separate parts on the sculpture: Two pieces of wood of almost equal thickness have been added, one on each side to compose a long bench that Christ is sitting on. These pieces have been attached to the main part with wooden pegs and animal glue. In addition, there are three more, very thin pieces /slices of wood added to the right hand side of the bench, which are attached with pegs.

Missing parts: All six highest tops of the crown are missing.

Cracks in timber: There is a very thin crack, which starts from the back of the crown and continues down the right side of the face until the corner of the eye. Another crack is visible on the back and the side of the left hand.

Linen or parchment visible: There is a piece of parchment glued to the back of the sculpture. There are also three pieces of parchment glued on to wood in the area of the crown. There is also parchment visible on the left side of the knee, on the side of the left hand and under the red areas of the garment. Also on the gilded orb in his hand. All the parchment has been recycled from a manuscript; there is writing in German on of all of the pieces of parchment. The writing is best seen in UV light. No linen cloth is visible.
