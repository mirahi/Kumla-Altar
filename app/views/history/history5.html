Annunciation

The Annunciation is the announcement by the angel Gabriel to the Virgin Mary that she would conceive through the power of the Holy Spirit and bear a son to be called Jesus, who would be a mighty ruler. The significance of the Annunciation in Christianity goes without saying: it is the first event in Mary’s life told in the Bible, it is when the incarnation of Christ is reckoned to have taken place, and holds great importance in the Church’s teaching on the conception of Jesus. Therefore, the theme of Annunciation enjoys prevalence in Christian art. While the three essential elements are the angel, the Virgin and the dove of the Holy Spirit descending towards her, as a popular theme during the Renaissance period, the annunciation is portrayed in many different ways by many different artists. The use of flowers is prevalent among those art works of the Annunciation, as the event took place in springtime, with lily being the main element symbolizing the purity of the Virgin.

When the wings of the altar are completely closed, the altar shows the Annunciation.

The Virgin Mary and the angel Gabriel each have their own wing, the Virgin Mary on the left and the Angel Gabriel on the right.

Both the Virgin Mary and the Angel Gabriel are standing on a pavement chequered diagonally in red and yellow. The background on both panels is red, with gold-shadowed stars. The Virgin with blond hair is in blue mantle with green lining, and red undergarment. She stands with raised hands before the reading desk. Behind her is a cupboard with open door for books.

The Angel Gabriel is holding a cross and is wearing white and green mantle. In the Angel´s panel, in upper right corner, God the Father is presented within a cloud band.

In both panels there are long winding banderols with angelic salutations in minuscule script.

Unfortunately the both panels are largely damaged and thus the great, detailed color effects are difficult to comprehend now.
