Decoration

The whole Kumla altar is elaborately decorated with different types of gilding and complicated painting technique, including glazing in surprisingly many areas.

Gold and silver leaf as well as composite leaf, called Zwischgold has been used both in water gilding and oil gilding technique. Where silver has been used, it is glazed almost everywhere. Zwischgold has been used in areas less visible to the viewer, for example the gilded sides of the sculptures, on architectonic buttresses with pinnacles on corpus and both inner wings as well as on the corpus and inner wings where patterns in oil gilding have been used. In most of the areas, Zwischgold has been considerably oxidized, due to a very thin layer of gold on a silver leaf.

Punch work patterns and other type of impression technique on gold and silver are giving an idea of the tools used to decorate the surface.

The ground layer on top of timber and under gilding and paint layers is most probably a chalk and animal glue mixture. It has been applied on to all surfaces visible to the viewer with few exceptions.

On sculptures there is no ground layer and no other decoration layers under the sculptures and on their backsides. There is also no ground layer on the tracery of the altar, where the gold leaf is used in oil gilding technique and the paint layer seems to be applied directly on to the wood with only an animal glue layer in between. The thickness of the ground layer varies between different areas.

There is a large transition in the style of art throughout the Middle Ages. In the beginning part of the Middle Ages, extremely subtle colors were being used, and the art itself was a more low-key style. It was meant to specifically express and help to understand the readings, but not much to express additional ideas. The later part of the Middle Ages saw a more bold dramatic use of colors and also an influential type of painting. In the Kumla altar-piece vivid colors are used and skillful use of different shades of same color can clearly be seen. The paint layers seem to be all as oil based paint.
