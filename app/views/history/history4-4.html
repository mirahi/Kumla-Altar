Tracery

The corpus of the Kumla altar is divided into three panels or sections, the central one having double width, separated from side sections by architectonic buttresses with pinnacles. Over each side section there is one canopy, over central section two canopies. They are three-sided, consisting of upper level of bar tracery, architectonically framed in a crenelated crown, and a lower level, also consisting of bar tracery but confined below by stilted round arches, three per canopy. In the round arches tracery there is a window- rose above and trefoil arch below. (Kuvat: Corpus 1,2,3,4,5)

At base of the corpus panel is a common socle, whose indrawn front consists of a graceful tracery formed as a series of profiled roundels with fish-bladder pattern, connected by rectangles with each other, cutting recumbent pointed ovals with quarterfoiled small rose ornaments. Behind the tracery there are thin pasteboards, alternating in red and blue.

The socle decoration recurs on insides of the inner wings. The canopy arrangement, simplified in the wings, reappears also in the centre part. The bar tracery at two levels recurs at a slightly broken angle, the lower row of bars forms the background of three somewhat stilted ogee arches with crockets and finial and rich fish-bladder tracery in openings of arch. Behind the bar tracery and socle bar tracery pasteboards alternating in red and blue have been placed to intensify colour. (Kuvat: Inner left wing 1,2,3,4,5)ja (Kuvat: Inner right wing 1,2,3,4)
