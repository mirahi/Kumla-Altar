Conservation and treatments

Cleaning wax

  • Surface dust was removed from all the surfaces with soft brush and vacuum cleaner. Especially the back sides of the sculptures had lots of surface dirt.
  • The wax and resin mixture on all the surfaces, used to consolidate the altar-piece in previous conservation treatment in 1950-s was removed both mechanically and with the help of petroleum benzene (boiling range 100-140 °C, naphtha benzene). (Kuvat: Cleaning Wax 1, 2 ja 3)

Cleaning

  • Cleaning of the remains of the wax was done with petroleum benzene (boiling range 100-140 °C, naphtha benzene), Fleming solution (ethanol 500ml, metylsalicylat 15ml, ammoniac 8ml) and with triammonium citrate and water (Kuvat: Cleaning 1 – 10)

Consolidation

  • Loose gesso ground, gilding and paint layers were consolidated using Lascaux Medium for Consolidation with the help of hot spatula and the excess of glue was removed with slightly moistened cotton wool. In many of the areas treated, consolidation was necessary prior to surface cleaning and wax removal. (Kuvat: Consolidation 1- 6)
  • Areas where surface protection paper had been attached to secure loose ground, gilding and paint were treated carefully either through the previously attached paper or after the paper had been removed. (Kuvat: Consolidation 7 – 9)
  • There were areas on the painted and gilded surfaces where ground, gilding and paint layers had serious detachment due to shrinkage of wooden support. All the areas were consolidated successfully. (Kuvat: Consolidation 10 – 14)

Varnishing

  • Hot spatula was used to further attach the areas of paint, which had been previously consolidated with wax -resin and were not in level with the rest of the surface.
  • Varnishing, before retouching, was done by brush with dammar resin varnish diluted with wood turpentine. Blue areas of paint containing azurite were not varnished because this paint is supposed to be matt. (Kuva: Varnishing, St Paul varnish before and after)

Retouching

  • Retouching was made using tratteggio technique with dry pigments in dammar that was diluted with wood turpentine. Additional wood turpentine was used to dilute the dammar varnish further in order to match more matt surface in some areas. The pigments used were Zink white, Raw Umber, Raw Sienna, Burnt Sienna and Bone Black. On the corpus and inner sides of the inner wings, retouching was made also in tratteggio technique but using watercolours. (Kuvat: Retouching 1-8)

Construction

Documentation in written and photographic form of the object under conservation including written documentation of all the conservation materials and procedures used is essential part of conservation-restoration.

Construction drawings of the altar, skillfully done by furniture conservation students and close up photographs were also part of the documentation. (Kuvat: Construction 1-5)
